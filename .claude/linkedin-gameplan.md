## Strategic Note: Company Page vs. Founder Page

**You need both — they do different jobs.**

| | Company Page (@TalentWharf) | Founder Page (your personal) |
|--|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| Reach | Lower (LinkedIn suppresses company pages) | 3–5x higher organic reach |
| Purpose | Brand credibility — people check you out before signing up | Growth engine — where attention is built |
| Tone | Brand voice, slightly more polished | Personal stories, raw takes |
| Best for | Product announcements, proof, feature moments | Belief posts, origin story, hot takes |

**The playbook:** Post the belief-heavy, story-driven content from your personal account. Post the product-forward, proof-based content from TalentWharf. Cross-post when relevant. The company page benefits from you tagging it in your personal posts.

If you can only do one right now: post everything from the company page, but write it like a founder, not a brand.

## Content Pillars (What TalentWharf Talks About)

| Pillar | % | What It Does | Example Angle |
|--------|---|-------------|---------------|
| **The Belief** | 35% | Builds a worldview. Attracts people who agree. | "Culture fit is established before you post the job" |
| **The Problem** | 30% | Makes readers feel seen. Creates urgency. | "Your best candidates messaged you months ago. You forgot." |
| **The Proof** | 20% | Shows the product creates real outcomes. | "Beta team found 8 people in their LinkedIn DMs they'd forgotten" |
| **The Build** | 10% | Creates authenticity + startup community reach | "Why we built AI matching before a search bar" |
| **The Invite** | 5% | Direct asks — only after earning attention | "We just opened 15 more beta spots" |

**Rule:** Never two "Invite" posts in a row. Never more than one promotional post per week. Earn the right to pitch by delivering value first.

## Post Format Guide

### Format 1: The Story (highest engagement)
```
[Specific, concrete hook — something that happened]

[Set the scene in 2-3 short lines]

[The turn — what went wrong or what you realized]

[The insight — what this means]

[Call to action or question]
```

### Format 2: The Hot Take
```
[Contrarian opener — bold claim]

[Why the conventional wisdom is wrong]

[What's actually true]

[What this means for your audience]

[Question to invite debate]
```

### Format 3: The List
```
[Hook: "X things Y people never tell you about Z"]

→ Point 1 (unexpected)
→ Point 2 (counterintuitive)
→ Point 3 (actionable)
→ Point 4 (memorable)

[Closing line that ties it together]
```

### Format 4: The Observation
```
[Notice something specific in the world]

[Expand on why it matters]

[Connect to the bigger belief]

[Short ending punch]
```

### LinkedIn Formatting Rules
- First line must work without reading anything else
- One sentence = one paragraph. Two max.
- No links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses these). Put links in first comment.
- End with a question or a very short sentence. Never end with the product pitch.
- Posts 150–300 words outperform longer posts for company pages.

**Post 1 — Monday**

```
The best hire you'll ever make probably already messaged you.

Most startups obsess over sourcing: job boards, LinkedIn recruiter,

The first message.

The problem isn't finding people like this. They find you.

How many of those messages are sitting in your DMs right now?
```

```
Not because those founders didn't care. Because they weren't hiring when

That's not a LinkedIn problem. That's a memory problem.
```

```
Hot take: the hiring funnel is backwards.
```

## Engagement Rules

**Always do:**
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours (even just "great point, [name]")
- Like and reply to people who share your posts
- When someone DMs about TalentWharf → send beta invite or waitlist link

**Never do:**
- Leave a post with 5+ comments unanswered
- Use auto-engagement tools
- Post promotional content without an earned "credit" from value posts
- Put the link in the post body (always first comment)

## Analytics to Check Weekly (LinkedIn Company Page)

Go to Analytics → Posts:
- **Impressions** — are they growing week over week?
- **Engagement rate** — comments are worth more than likes. Target >2%.
- **Follower growth** — note which posts drove new follows
- **Top performing post** — what format? What pillar? Replicate.

**Decision rule:** If a post gets 2x average impressions → write a follow-up post from a different angle on the same topic. The algorithm told you people want that content.
